## What this attacks
The margin needs one-sided control of deep-branch frequency `N_k = #{j : c'_j ≡ 1/3 mod 2^k}`. Two terms in the
same deep cylinder **collide**: `c'_i ≡ c'_j mod 2^k ⟺ v₂(c'_i − c'_j) ≥ k`. So `N_k` is governed by the orbit's
**self-separation** `v₂(c'_i − c'_j)`. `EXPERT_ASK` Q2 asked: does **p-adic Baker** (linear forms in 2-adic logs)
give an *unconditional* lower bound on `v₂(c'_i − c'_j)`, hence a separation bound, hence an `N_k` bound — the one
route to an unconditional partial (everything else is conditional/characterization).

## Two findings
**(1) The orbit separates exactly like random — nothing to exploit.** Over ~4·10⁵ random pairs (J=4000 renewal
terms, residues mod `2⁴⁰`), `P(v₂(c'_i−c'_j) ≥ k)` matches the random `2^{−k}` to ratio `0.91–1.01` for all `k`;
max collision depth 16 sits at the birthday scale `log₂(#pairs) ≈ 18.6`. No better-than-random separation exists
(a fourth independent confirmation of i.i.d.-likeness, now from the Diophantine/separation side).

**(2) Baker is inapplicable at the source — rigorously.** The direct orbit `c_n = ⌊3c_{n−1}/2⌋` satisfies the
**verified exact identity**
```
c_n = (3^n c_0 − T_n) / 2^n,    T_n = Σ_{i<n} 3^{n−1−i} 2^i r_i,    r_i = (3 c_i) mod 2 ∈ {0,1}
```
(`T_n` = the dual carry of Prop 4; identity machine-checked through `n=600`). Crucially **`height(T_n) ≈ n·log₂3 =
1.585 n` grows linearly** (verified bit-lengths: `5,13,77,156,473,949` at `n=5,10,50,100,300,600`). So `c_n` — and
hence any difference `c_i − c_j` — is a **nested-floor value of unbounded height**, *not* a bounded-height S-unit

algebraic numbers; here the relevant "numbers" are the `T_n`, whose height grows with `n`. **Baker therefore cannot
apply — structurally, not for lack of effort.**
