## The unification (the single reason they all fail)
Every engine that can prove a **single** orbit equidistributes needs one of:
1. a **second multiplicatively-independent direction** (rank ≥ 2) — *Fourier/BFLM, entropy, additive, rigidity*;
2. a **unipotent** structure — *Ratner* — which `×(3/2)` does not have; or
3. it delivers only **`a.e.` / the `Θ(log N)` foothold** — *transfer operator, Weyl*.

The rank-1 cyclic orbit `⟨×(3/2)⟩` provides **none** of these. And the four phrasings we met —
> "**self-duality**" (BFLM) · "**missing recurrence**" (entropy) · "**no spectral gap**" (additive) ·
> "**no `×2,×3` pair**" (rigidity)

— are **all the same fact**:
> ### The orbit carries only the rank-1 combination `×(3/2)`, never the rank-2 pair `×2, ×3` separately.
The map factors as `×3` then `÷2`, but the *orbit* only ever sees the composite; the two primes are never
available as independent directions. That single deficiency is the entire wall, seen through seven tools.

## The rank-2 search inside the renewal structure — RESOLVED: the hidden non-abelianness is AMENABLE
The last constructive hope was to recover an effective rank-2 from the renewal decomposition (`T = ×3` then
`÷2`). **Result: no — and the precise reason deepens the wall to AMENABILITY** (`renewal_rank2.py`).
- The renewal map `F` is generated by the **branch maps** `f_D(x) = (3/2)^{D+1} x + s_D`, with shifts
  `s_D = (2^D−3^D)/2^{D+1} ∈ ℤ[1/6]` (involving *both* primes). These are genuinely **non-abelian**: verified
  `f_0∘f_1 ≠ f_1∘f_0` (same linear part `27/8`, shifts `−3/8` vs `−1/4`).
- **But the group they generate is SOLVABLE:** linear parts `{(3/2)^k}` form a rank-1 abelian group; the
  translations `ℤ[1/6]` form an abelian normal subgroup; the whole sits in `ℤ[1/6] ⋊ ⟨3/2⟩` (metabelian).
- **Solvable ⇒ amenable ⇒ no spectral gap / no expansion.** Bourgain–Gamburd / BFLM expansion needs a
  **non-amenable (semisimple / Zariski-dense in semisimple)** group. The renewal's "second direction" (the
  translations) is abelian and amenable — the **wrong kind**. So **no effective rank-2 is recovered.**
- **Deepened characterization of the wall:** the orbit's entire effective symmetry — whether the cyclic `⟨×3/2⟩`
  or the larger renewal affine semigroup `ℤ[1/6] ⋊ ⟨3/2⟩` — is **amenable**. Every single-orbit equidistribution
  engine needs **non-amenability** (a semisimple/expanding second direction), which the problem fundamentally
  lacks. **The wall is amenability.** (This is why non-amenable, semisimple, rank-≥2 actions enjoy rigidity and
  ours does not — a single principle behind all seven tools.)

## The sharpest form: the wall is `amenable ∩ hyperbolic` (a rank-1 Anosov single orbit)
Why are the *amenable* cousins (rotation `{nα}`, odometer `x+1`) tractable while our (also amenable) `×(3/2)` is
not? Because they are **isometric** (`|·|=1` everywhere) or **contracting-dominated** (Pisot). `×(3/2)` is
**genuinely hyperbolic** — expanding at `∞,2`, contracting at `3`, product `1`, real stable+unstable manifolds:
a **rank-1 Anosov automorphism** of the solenoid. So:
- **amenable + isometric** (rotation/odometer) ⇒ Weyl / unique ergodicity — tractable;
- **non-amenable + hyperbolic** (rank-≥2 Cat-map-type) ⇒ measure rigidity — tractable;
- **amenable + hyperbolic** (`×(3/2)`, rank-1 Anosov) ⇒ **no tool** — the wall.
The wall is precisely the **intersection** `amenable ∩ hyperbolic`: amenability kills the rigidity engines,
hyperbolicity kills the rotation/Weyl engines, and a *single specified orbit* of a rank-1 Anosov automorphism is
exactly the empty spot in the toolbox. (This is the open single-orbit problem for a hyperbolic toral/solenoid
automorphism — the dynamical heart of Mahler 3/2.)
